< Akan name

(/əˈkæn/) are a meta-ethnicity living in the southern regions of present-day Ghana, formerly known as the Gold Coast.

There is an Akan maxim ne din ne ne honam sε (his/her name befits his/her body).

The Akans call it kradin (lit.) ‘souls name’ and they believe that this is a name that a person’s soul offers him/her.

Ghana‘s first president, Kwame Nkrumah, was born on a Saturday

the seventh Secretary-General of the United Nations, Kofi Atta Annan, was so named for being born on a Friday.

The 25-year-old popular Ghanaian-British fashion model Adwoa Caitlin Maria Aboah is Monday-born.
